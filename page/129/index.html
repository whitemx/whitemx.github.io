change

we have been using webgain’s visual cafe
for our java development for the last 18 months. we already knew that they had
been bought out by togethersoft.
now, we had been talking to togethersoft to get new licenses for visual cafe
but had been put off by the ludicrous price (gbp8,000 per license) and now we
discover that borland have
bought them out and are planning to get rid of the product altogether! so the
upshot is that we have to find a new ide.

after much looking around the concensus seems to be fixing around eclipse which is the open-source product
started by ibm. so far it looks pretty good, and we always have the option to
spend more money and get into websphere application developer (which is eclipse
on steroids) if the planned jsp development takes off.

it’s just a matter of learning a whole set of new ide quirks (or bugs as they
are known to everyone else)

testing

i have just been testing my connectivity setup for the laptop while i am away.
rather amazingly it all seems to be working as planned. as i have mentioned
before, i have subscribed to net2roam.
this allows me to dial-up using a modem from anywhere in the world at local
call rates. (although, hopefully i won’t need to use it too much as a lot of
hotels now advertise that they have broadband in their rooms.) so once i am
connected, using the wonders of lotus
notes i can replicate all of my mail and blog entries etc in a compressed
format. mail can be written off-line.

all this means that i should be able to keep in touch with people while i am
away, and, of course, update this site with what i am up to and some photos.
